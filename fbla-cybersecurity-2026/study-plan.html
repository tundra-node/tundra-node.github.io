Total today: ~3.5 hrs
Day 2 — Wednesday (Operations + Risk)
  • ⚡Incident Response Lifecycle — NIST SP 800-61 phases: Preparation → Detection & Analysis → Containment/Eradication/Recovery → Post-Incident. Know the order cold.45m
  • ⚡DR/BCP Mastery — RPO vs RTO (RPO = data loss tolerance, RTO = downtime tolerance). Hot/Warm/Cold site RTOs. BCP covers ALL business functions; DRP covers only IT.60m
  • 📝Risk Management Math — SLE = AV × EF, ALE = SLE × ARO. Know Accept/Avoid/Mitigate/Transfer. Quantitative vs Qualitative.30m
  • 📝Quiz Drill — 30 questions from "Security Operations and Management." Focus on firewall types, ACLs, SIEM.45m
  • 🃏Forensics Drill — Order of Volatility, dd command, fsck, chain of custody.30m
💡 NLC trick: They often say "RPO measures downtime" — that's FALSE. RPO measures DATA LOSS. RTO measures DOWNTIME. Mixing these up is their #1 trap.
Total today: ~3.5 hrs
Day 3 — Thursday (Design + Threats)
  • ⚡RAID Levels — RAID 0 = striping (no redundancy), RAID 1 = mirroring, RAID 5 = striping + distributed parity (min 3 disks, survives 1 failure), RAID 10 = striping + mirroring (min 4 disks).30m
  • ⚡Cloud Models — IaaS (most control, you manage OS+), PaaS (provider manages OS, you manage app+data), SaaS (provider manages all). Know examples of each.30m
  • 📝TCSEC Rainbow Series — Orange Book = TCSEC (rated D/C1/C2/B1/B2/B3/A1). Red Book = Network Security. Green Book = Password Management. Know what A1 means (verified design).40m
  • 📝IoT Security — Default credentials, limited hardware, inability to patch, no auto-update. Know mantraps/Faraday cages for physical security.25m
  • 📝Full Mock Exam #1 — 100 questions, 50 minutes. Score yourself brutally.55m
  • 📝Review Missed Questions — Go through every wrong answer. Read the explanation.45m
💡 High-yield: The NLC tests RAID 5 description ("striping with distributed parity") and cloud model distinctions constantly. Don't skip these.
Total today: ~4 hrs
Day 4 — Friday (Laws + Mitigation + Full Review)
  • ⚡Laws & Compliance — HIPAA (healthcare PHI), FERPA (student records), COPPA (children under 13), GDPR (4% global revenue fines), SOX (financial reporting), PCI DSS (cardholder data), CFAA (unauthorized access). Know which protects what.45m
  • ⚡TCSEC Deeper Dive — ITSEC (E0-E6), Common Criteria (EAL1-EAL7). What replaced what. Orange Book = TCSEC. Order: C1 → C2 → B1 → B2 → B3 → A1 (increasing security).40m
  • 📝Historical Malware — Morris Worm (1988, sendmail+fingerd), Melissa (1999, macro), ILOVEYOU (2000, VBScript), Code Red (2001, IIS), Nimda (2001, multi-vector), MyDoom (2004, fastest email worm), Storm (2007, P2P botnet).35m
  • 📝IDS vs IPS vs Firewall vs WAF — IDS = detect + alert, IPS = detect + block, WAF = Layer 7 web app protection, firewall = Layers 3-4 packet filtering.25m
  • 📝Full Mock Exam #2 — 100 questions, 50 minutes. Target: 85%+.55m
  • 🃏Flashcard Blitz — 50 cards from all categories.30m
💡 NLC trap: They'll phrase laws as "Which of the following BEST describes..." and give you two partially correct answers. COPPA is about children under 13 (not 16). FERPA protects student records (not just grades — ALL records).
